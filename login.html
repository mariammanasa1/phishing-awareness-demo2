This was a phishing-awareness simulation

No file was downloaded, no code was run, and no personal or device data was collected.

The email used curiosity about a car photo to make the click feel harmless. Real attacks often disguise risky links as photos, invoices, delivery notices, or shared documents.

  • Pause before opening unexpected photo links or attachments.
  • Check whether the sender is a known facilities contact.
  • Look for missing details, such as location, ticket number, vehicle description, or official channel.
  • Report suspicious messages through the approved internal process.

When something feels off, verify it through a trusted channel instead of clicking.
